### The Translation Manipulation

**The Capitalization Problem:**

Original Greek manuscripts:
- Written entirely in capitals (ΥΙΟΣΘΕΟΥ = UIOS THEOU)
- No punctuation, no spaces between words
- No way to distinguish common noun from proper title
- Context alone determined meaning

**What Translators Did:**
- Added capitalization based on theological assumptions
- "son of god" became "Son of God" when referring to Jesus
- Same phrase stayed lowercase when referring to others
- Created exclusive divine claim through punctuation choice

**The Dishonest Pattern:**
This capitalization manipulation serves a very particular readership - those already committed to Christian theological claims. It can be deceiving for people outside that interpretive tradition. That's life, and that's why it's called the "Christian Bible" - it tells the Christian take on scriptures they selected from among many others and how they interpret them.

**The Honest Reality:**
In original texts, there is no grammatical or linguistic marker making Jesus's "son of god" status different from anyone else called "son of god" (which was common language for righteous people, kings, and spiritually developed individuals).
