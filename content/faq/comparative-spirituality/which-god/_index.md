# Which God? Understanding the Divine in Wayist Teaching

## The Foundation Problem

**Western monotheism has collapsed three fundamentally different realities into one word, causing millions of genuine seekers to abandon the divine entirely when they leave a religion that taught a violent, tribal deity** — not realizing they have only rejected one specific being, not the divine as such.

## The Discovery Process (Divine Reality Recognition)

**The Consistency Test:** *"If 'God' referred to a single being with a consistent nature, descriptions of that being would be consistent across traditions — but they are not. What does that inconsistency tell you about whether these traditions are describing the same being?"*

**The Jesus Distinction:** *"Jesus explicitly told the religious authorities of his time that their god and his Father in Heaven were not the same being — calling the former a murderer and liar. If the central figure Christians claim as authority drew this distinction himself, what does it mean that Christianity spent two thousand years collapsing it?"*

**The Departure Question:** *"When someone says 'I don't believe in God anymore,' are they rejecting the divine — or are they rejecting one specific, well-marketed deity? What would it mean to you if those were different things?"*

## The Wayist Foundation (Three Distinct Realities)

### Quick Answer (30 seconds)

**"God" in common usage conflates three distinct things: the incomprehensible Absolute, the benign spirit deities of Sukhavati, and the tribal soul-gods of certain scriptures.** They are not the same being. Spirit deities are by nature benign, non-violent, and non-tribal. Any deity described as jealous, genocidal, or tribalistic is not a spirit deity — and recognizing this distinction is the beginning of theological clarity.

### The Key Teaching

**IF** spirit deities are by nature benign, non-violent, and non-tribalistic **THEN** any deity described in scripture as jealous, genocidal, demanding blood sacrifice, and drowning entire civilisations is not a spirit deity — whatever else it may be.

**IF** Jesus identified his Father in Heaven as distinct from the deity of Jewish scripture — calling the latter a murderer and liar — **THEN** any tradition that merges these two beings into one "God" directly contradicts the central figure it claims as authority.

**IF** Paul of Tarsus attached the entire Hebrew Bible to Jesus's teaching and merged these different beings into one "God" **THEN** the theological confusion this created is not incidental — it is the engineered product of a specific historical decision that has caused immeasurable harm.

**IF** millions of people are leaving religion while still believing in something spiritual beyond the physical world **THEN** they are not abandoning the divine — they are rejecting one specific deity, and nobody has told them there are others.

### The Three Levels

Wayism distinguishes three distinct realities that Western theology collapses into one:

**The Absolute — THAT:**
The incomprehensible and ineffable source of all existence. Beyond the scope of human understanding or description. As the Upanishads declare: *Neti, neti* — "Not this, not that." No concept can capture Its essence. It is not a person. It does not have emotions, preferences, or tribal loyalties. It cannot be prayed to in the conventional sense because it is not separate from you. [*Primary Teaching*, Ch. 13]

**God our Father and God our Mother in Heaven — Spirit Deities:**
God our Father (Amitabha, "Bright Light King of Heaven") and God our Mother (Pandarajanani, "Jasmin Light Mother of Heaven") are the benign spirit beings who oversee soul development in Sukhavati, our spirit heaven. By nature: non-violent, non-tribal, non-judgmental. They do not demand worship, command genocide, or play favourites with one people. They function as the wisest possible parents overseeing an educational institution — designing learning experiences, supporting growth, and demonstrating what we can become. They don't punish failure; they provide another opportunity to learn. [*Primary Teaching*, Ch. 17, Ch. 79]

**Soul-gods of Tribal Religion:**
Anthropomorphised beings reflecting human qualities — jealousy, rage, favouritism, demands for blood sacrifice, commands for genocide, and eternal punishment for disobedience. These are soul-religion constructions. Whatever they are, they are not spirit deities. The practical test: spiritual beings are free from tribalism, speciesism, bigotry, genocidal notions, intimidation, and the need to inflict hardship. Any being exhibiting these characteristics — regardless of which tradition claims it as supreme — fails that test. [*theWAY of Wholesomeness*]

## How Wayism Differs From Other Traditions

### Jesus Made This Distinction Himself

When the Jewish religious leaders claimed God as their father, Jesus said their father was not his Father — that their deity was a murderer from the beginning, a liar who speaks out of his own character. His Father in Heaven was something else entirely [*Jesus the Wayist*, Ch. 12; cf. John 8:44].

This is not a minor footnote. The central figure of the most influential spiritual movement in history drew a bright line between two fundamentally different deities — and Christianity spent two thousand years pretending he didn't.

### The Paul Synthesis and Its Consequences

Paul of Tarsus, who never met Jesus during his teaching years, attached the entire Hebrew Bible as the foundation document of his new religion — merging the benign Father Jesus spoke of with the violent, jealous tribal deity of Jewish scripture, and calling them the same God. Three fundamentally different concepts — the Absolute, a spirit deity, and a tribal soul-god — forced into one word.

The result is a theological incoherence that thoughtful people can feel even if they can't name it: a deity who is simultaneously described as unconditional love and as commanding the extermination of entire peoples, including infants and livestock. These cannot be the same being. The confusion is what drives genuine moral people out of religion — not a failure of faith but a functioning moral compass recognizing that something does not add up.

### The "Which Buddha?" Parallel

The same principle applies to Buddhism. Buddhist scripture describes billions of heavenly beings, countless buddhas across countless heavens. Gautama is one buddha among many. The very core of Buddhist teaching holds that we are all on the path to becoming buddhas ourselves. Yet "the Buddha" has become a singular noun in Western usage, collapsing the same vast spiritual reality into a bumper sticker. Lord Siddhartha Gautama, Lord Jesus, Lord Krishna, Teacher LaoTzi — among those known to human history who graduated the Butterfly Path and became spirit deities. [*Primary Teaching*, Ch. 79; *Butterfly Path*, 2nd Ed.]

## Practical Application Framework

### Asking the Question in Daily Life

**"God bless our troops" before destroying a civilian population:** Which God? The benign Father of Jesus' teaching would never bless slaughter. The tribal deity of Hebrew scripture commanded exactly this. The politician invokes the latter while borrowing the warmth of the former. Recognizing this removes the moral confusion — and the religious authority claimed for the act.

**"God loves you" followed by threats of eternal torture:** Which God? A being that loves unconditionally and a being that tortures for eternity cannot be the same being. The statement is incoherent unless two different beings have been merged. They have been.

**"God has a plan" to a grieving parent:** Which God? If the tribal deity, yes — that deity's "plans" include children dying as punishment or test. If the benign Father and Mother of Wayist teaching, the understanding is different: the Law of Karma assigns soul curriculum, and while this life's pain is real and deserves compassion, it is part of a much larger educational journey that no single deity "planned" as punishment. [*Jesus the Wayist*, Ch. 16]

### For Seekers Who Left Religion

If you left because you couldn't accept a deity who commands genocide and eternal punishment — you were right to leave. Your moral compass is working. That revulsion is your higher-self recognizing that something in that teaching doesn't add up.

But you may not have left the divine. Not believing in one particular deity is like visiting one bad restaurant and deciding food doesn't exist. The experience was real and the rejection makes sense, but the conclusion is too broad.

Spirit deities — God our Father and God our Mother in Heaven — are of a fundamentally different nature. They don't need your worship. They don't demand sacrifice. They don't play favourites. They don't punish mistakes. They guide, support, educate, and love — because that is their nature, the way water flows downhill. [*Jesus the Wayist*, Ch. 16; *Primary Teaching*, Ch. 79]

### For Spiritual Development

Recognizing the distinction between spirit deities and soul-gods is itself a mark of spirit-mind development. The capacity for this discernment — to test a claim about the divine against what spirit deities are actually like by nature — is exactly the kind of logical spiritual skill the Butterfly Path develops. Every prayer, by whichever name the devotee may call, is answered by one's Divine Tara. [*Primary Teaching*, Ch. 79] The divine does not require the correct label. It requires honest engagement.

## Common Follow-Up Questions

**Q: Doesn't this make Wayism anti-Christian or anti-Jewish?**
A: No. Wayism considers Jesus one of the great graduates of the Butterfly Path — a spirit deity now in Sukhavati. The critique is of the Pauline conflation of different beings, not of people in those traditions. Many sincere Christians connect with the benign Father Jesus taught about, not the tribal deity of certain scriptures, without ever articulating the distinction.

**Q: How do I know which deity someone is invoking when they say "God"?**
A: Apply the practical test. Spirit deities are by nature benign, non-violent, non-tribal. If the deity being described or invoked commands violence, demands tribal loyalty, punishes non-members, or rewards aggression — that is not a spirit deity, regardless of what the invoker claims.

**Q: Does this mean the tribal deity doesn't exist?**
A: Wayism's position is that soul-entities of various kinds exist in the Soul Domain. The question isn't whether the being described in certain scriptures exists but what kind of being it is — and whether it deserves the title of supreme divine reality. By Wayist understanding, it does not.

**Q: If God our Father and God our Mother don't need worship, why connect with them?**
A: We connect not to appease beings who require our worship but to align ourselves with beings whose nature is wisdom and compassion. The connection serves our development and expresses our gratitude. Your Divine Tara — your personal spiritual guide from among those spirit beings — is already present. The relationship is available regardless of which religious label you hold.

### The Ultimate Truth About Divine Reality

The word "God" has been made to carry too many contradictory meanings at once. No wonder people are confused. No wonder they're leaving. And no wonder that even as they leave, something in them continues to believe there is more to existence than the physical.

"Which God?" is not the end of a spiritual conversation. It's the starting point. It gives the person who left religion permission to keep searching without guilt. It gives the person still inside a tool for examining what they've been told versus what rings true in their own developing spiritual awareness. It gives all of us — in a world where "God" is invoked to justify everything from charity to genocide — a way to cut through the noise.

The benign Father and Mother in Heaven, the incomprehensible Absolute, the Butterfly Path toward becoming what they are — none of this requires a specific religious label, institutional membership, or the correct answer to a theological examination. It requires only honest engagement with what is actually true.
