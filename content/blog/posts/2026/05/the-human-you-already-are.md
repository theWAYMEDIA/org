# The Human You Already Are

*Reclaiming the Body's Own Intelligence*

---

Watch someone's morning.

The alarm sounds — a synthetic tone from a device charging on the nightstand, its screen having leaked blue light into the room all night. Feet hit the floor and slide into slippers. They will not touch the earth today. The bathroom cabinet opens onto a small pharmacy of interventions: antibacterial soap, synthetic-fragranced shampoo, conditioner, moisturiser, deodorant with aluminium compounds, aftershave or perfume, sunscreen. Each product addresses a problem that the previous product helped create, or that advertising invented entirely. The skin — a living ecosystem hosting hundreds of bacterial species in a signature as unique as a fingerprint — is systematically stripped and recoated with petrochemical derivatives before the person has eaten breakfast.

Breakfast is a cereal whose flour was bleached with chlorine dioxide, whose dough was conditioned with azodicarbonamide, whose shelf life was extended with BHT, and whose taste was engineered in a laboratory to produce a craving cycle that ensures the next purchase. Or it is skipped entirely, replaced by coffee from a pod — aluminium capsule, plastic liner, roasted months ago, flavoured with synthetic vanillin.

The commute happens in shoes, in a vehicle, under artificial light. The body will not feel sunlight on the skin for hours. By the time it does, it will be filtered through glass or blocked by chemical sunscreen. The circadian system — the body's master clock that has been setting cortisol and melatonin timing by the angle of morning light for three hundred million years of vertebrate evolution — receives no signal. It guesses. It guesses wrong.

This is not a description of a careless person. This is a description of a normal day in the modern world. Every element of it has been sold to us as progress, as hygiene, as self-care. And nearly every element interferes with a system that was already working.

This series has spent four articles mapping those systems. This final essay brings them home.

## Before the Day Begins

The body's first need upon waking is light. Not the light from a screen. Not the light from an overhead LED. The body needs the full-spectrum light of the morning sky — even on a cloudy day, outdoor light delivers orders of magnitude more lux than any indoor source. When this light enters the eyes within the first hour of waking, it triggers a cascade the body has been running since long before humans existed: melanopsin ganglion cells in the retina signal the suprachiasmatic nucleus, the brain's master clock. Cortisol releases — the healthy morning pulse that provides alertness, focus, and immune function. Simultaneously, a sixteen-hour timer begins for the evening release of melatonin, the hormone that will bring sleep when darkness arrives.

Miss this light, and the cortisol pulse drifts later in the day. The consequence is not dramatic on any single morning. It is cumulative — a slow dysregulation that shows up as difficulty falling asleep, low-grade anxiety, afternoon fatigue, and a general sense of being slightly out of rhythm with your own biology. The research from Stanford and the National Institutes of Mental Health is clear: the single most impactful daily behaviour for sleep quality, mood regulation, and hormonal timing is morning sunlight exposure. Five to ten minutes, outdoors, without sunglasses. The body does the rest.

Then the hum.

This series began with [The Science of Humming OM](/blog/posts/2026/05/the-science-of-humming-om/) — five simultaneous physiological mechanisms activated by a closed mouth and a sustained note. Sinus nitric oxide flushed into the airstream. Vagus nerve mechanically stimulated through vocal cord vibration. Exhale-dominant breathing ratio enforced without counting. Cerebrospinal fluid oscillated. Glymphatic waste clearance possibly enhanced. Sixty seconds. No equipment. No training. No subscription.

Never not hum your OMs. You and everyone who lives in and on your body — the trillions of microbial residents whose collective mind manufactures your dopamine, processes your food, trains your immune cells, and influences your mood — you all need that. The hum is not just for you. It is for the entire civilisation that your body houses. The parasympathetic shift it produces, the nitric oxide it delivers, the vagal tone it maintains — these serve every system, every resident, every mind.

And then, if you can: bare feet on the earth.

For all of human history until very recently, the first sensation of the day was contact between skin and ground. The research on grounding is still developing, but the direction is consistent: direct barefoot contact with the earth's surface normalises cortisol rhythms, reduces blood viscosity, decreases inflammatory markers, and improves sleep quality. The proposed mechanism — that free electrons from the earth's surface act as natural antioxidants when absorbed through the skin — is plausible and supported by multiple peer-reviewed studies, though the field is young. What is not debatable is that the human foot evolved to touch the ground, and for the vast majority of human existence, it did. The modern shoe — rubber sole, synthetic insole, sealed away from any contact with the surface of the planet — is an experiment we have been running for less than a century. The body has been barefoot for millions of years.

Morning light. The OM hum. Feet on the earth. Cool water on the face — the Dayspring practice that Wayists share across all cultures, the physical gesture of opening the senses to the day. Four acts. None of them costs anything. None of them requires a product. All of them serve systems that were already running before you opened your eyes.

## The Skin You Live In

Now consider what you put on it.

The average person applies between six and twelve personal care products before leaving the house. Each one contains synthetic fragrance — the word "fragrance" on a label being a legal umbrella that can conceal dozens of undisclosed chemicals, protected as trade secrets. The International Fragrance Association's 2025 transparency list includes over 3,300 fragrance ingredients. Among the most common are phthalates and synthetic musks — both established endocrine disruptors.

Phthalates interfere with androgen and thyroid receptors. They are linked to reproductive system disruption, metabolic dysfunction, and developmental effects in children exposed prenatally. Synthetic musks accumulate in human tissue and breast milk. They are detectable in virtually every person tested in industrialised countries. An Environmental Working Group study found that seventy-two percent of products tested containing the ingredient "fragrance" contained phthalates. Up to ninety-five percent of synthetic fragrance chemicals derive from petrochemicals.

These chemicals do not stay on the skin. They volatilise into indoor air. They are absorbed through the skin and detected in urine within hours. They permeate clothing, bedding, furniture. The cumulative daily exposure from laundry detergent, fabric softener, dryer sheets, shampoo, conditioner, body wash, moisturiser, deodorant, perfume or cologne — layered together across every surface the skin touches — creates a chemical burden the body was never designed to process.

But here is the part that connects to everything this series has mapped: the synthetic fragrance is not just a toxic burden. It is a communication blackout.

The fourth article in this series, [The Chemistry Your Body Already Reads](/blog/posts/2026/05/the-chemistry-your-body-already-reads/), described the molecular language the body speaks through its skin — pheromones, MHC immune signalling, microbiome metabolites, volatile organic compounds that communicate identity, health, genetic compatibility, and emotional state to every other human who comes close enough to sense them. This chemical signature is as individual as your face. It is what makes you recognisable, attractive, readable to the people around you — and particularly to an intimate partner, whose body is performing molecular assessments through taste and smell that have been guiding human pair-bonding since before the species had language.

Synthetic fragrance replaces this signature with a commercial product. It does not enhance your scent. It erases it. The body that approaches you can no longer read your immune compatibility, your microbiome health, your hormonal state. The ancient reading systems — the olfactory-limbic pathway, the MHC evaluation, the vagal taste response — encounter information-free noise where a biological signal should be.

We have made ourselves chemically illegible to each other. And the industry that did this sells it as attractiveness.

The alternative is not complicated. Wash with real soap — the kind made from fat and lye, the way soap has been made for thousands of years. Use it where you need it — armpits, groin, feet — and leave the rest of your skin alone most of the time. The microbiome recovers. The sebum returns. The bacterial populations rebalance. The skin begins doing what it has always done: protecting, signalling, breathing, communicating.

Let your body smell like you. The almost imperceptible scent that is yours and no one else's — the scent that a lover recognises in the dark, that a child finds comforting, that communicates your identity to every living thing that encounters it — is not something to be masked. It is something to be reclaimed.

## What Goes In

The body that hums OM, that walks barefoot, that wears its own scent — that body also needs to eat. And here, the same principle applies: what is already available is almost always what the body actually needs.

Bread made from flour, water, salt, and time. Not bread made from flour, water, salt, azodicarbonamide, potassium bromate, calcium peroxide, DATEM, sodium stearoyl lactylate, monoglycerides, calcium sulphate, ammonium chloride, and several ingredients you would need a chemistry degree to pronounce. The first bread sustains a civilisation of gut bacteria. The second assaults it.

The gut microbiome shapes what happens when the deity — you — feeds its civilisation well. The bacteria ferment the fibre from real food and produce short-chain fatty acids that fuel the gut lining, regulate inflammation, and send signals along the vagus nerve to the brain that influence mood, cognition, and immune function. These bacteria manufacture serotonin and dopamine. They train immune cells. They are, in the Wayist understanding, one of the three body-minds — the microbiome-mind, a collective intelligence of billions of beings who co-inhabit the body and whose happiness or misery cascades through every other mind in the system.

When the microbiome-mind is unhappy, nobody shall be happy. This is not philosophy. It is biology. A disrupted gut produces neuroinflammation, mood dysregulation, immune dysfunction, skin conditions, and cognitive impairment. A thriving gut produces the precursors of contentment. The difference between the two is largely determined by what you feed it.

Fermented foods — the kimchi, the sauerkraut, the kefir, the yoghurt, the miso, the naturally fermented pickles — deliver living bacterial cultures directly to the gut ecosystem. Real bread, made with natural leaven and slow fermentation, is partially pre-digested by the bacteria in the starter, making nutrients more bioavailable and reducing the inflammatory compounds that fast industrial processing leaves intact. Pasta made from flour, egg, and water. Vegetables that still have soil on them. Fats from animals that ate what animals eat. Proteins that were alive recently enough to still carry nutritional integrity.

None of this is exotic. None of it requires a health food store. It requires the kitchen, the market, and the willingness to prepare food the way it was prepared for the entire history of the species until about seventy years ago. It is udbhu-kanti in its most literal expression — the beauty and sufficiency of what is simple, real, and enough.

## What the Skin Needs From the Sky

Sunlight on the skin does something this series has been tracking since the first article: it produces nitric oxide.

Research from the University of Edinburgh and elsewhere has demonstrated that UVA radiation — the component of sunlight that penetrates glass poorly and is blocked by most sunscreens — triggers the release of nitric oxide from stores in the skin. The skin contains nitrite at concentrations roughly twenty-five times higher than blood plasma. When UVA light reaches the skin, it releases this stored nitric oxide into circulation, producing vasodilation and lowering blood pressure through the identical molecular pathway this series mapped in [The Molecule Your Body Already Makes](/blog/posts/2026/05/humming-om-blood-pressure-and-erectile-health/) — the NO-cGMP-smooth muscle relaxation cascade.

The effect persists for hours after exposure, and may continue for days. The researchers note that this mechanism may be especially beneficial for the elderly and those with impaired nitric oxide function — precisely the populations most likely to have been told to avoid sun exposure entirely.

The modern relationship with sunlight has been deformed by fear. Legitimate concerns about skin cancer from chronic overexposure have been commercialised into a terror industry selling chemical sunscreens — many containing their own endocrine disruptors — and promoting total sun avoidance as a health strategy. The result is a population deficient in vitamin D, deficient in the circadian signalling that morning light provides, and now, we learn, deficient in the skin-produced nitric oxide that moderate sun exposure delivers.

Balance is the body's language. Moderate sun exposure — morning light on the skin, sensible time outdoors without chemical barriers, avoiding the burn that signals damage — is not a risk to be managed. It is a biological requirement that the body has been meeting for the entire history of terrestrial life.

## The Gourmet Meal

Now the evening. And what this series has been building toward across five articles.

Sexual intimacy, in a long partnership, can narrow. The life narrows it — exhaustion, routine, familiarity, the compression of time. What was once an event of full-bodied exploration becomes genital-focused, orgasm-chasing, efficient. The body's most sophisticated sensory convergence reduced to a sprint toward a conclusion.

Everything this series has mapped argues against this narrowing.

The vagus nerve that runs from throat to genitals responds to vocalization — the moans, the sounds, the vibrations that most people suppress in the interest of speed or self-consciousness. The skin's chemical signature is being read through taste and smell — the pheromones, the microbiome metabolites, the MHC immune signals that communicate at a depth no words can reach. The olfactory-limbic pathway delivers the scent of a partner directly to the emotional brain without filtration. Oxytocin flows with bonding touch, with skin-to-skin contact, with the unhurried closeness that a thirty-minute encounter permits and a five-minute encounter does not. Nitric oxide, produced in the sinuses during any vocalization and released from the skin during warm contact, supports the vascular mechanics that arousal requires.

This is a gourmet meal. The most exquisite one the body is capable of preparing. Every ingredient is already present. Every system is already in place. The only thing missing, usually, is time and permission — time to taste, to smell, to touch slowly, to let the sounds arise, to let the chemistry do what it has been doing since before the species existed. And permission to let the experience be what it is: a near-spiritual event, arguably the closest most people will come to the divine through their senses.

The udbhu-kanti text asks: "When you make love, do you do it with your body or primarily your soul and heart?" The answer the body gives, when permitted, is: all of it. Every mind. Every system. The sacral mind reading the partner's chemistry. The throat mind extracting the wisdom of the experience. The heart mind bridging the physical to the spiritual. The microbiome-mind responding to the hormonal cascade. The organ-mind — the heart itself, the skin, the genitals — doing what they were designed to do at the level of tissue and nerve and blood.

Stop chasing the conclusion. Inhabit the meal.

## The Minds in Partnership

This series has explored how Svadhisthana and Vishuddhi — the sacral and throat minds — work together during intimacy: the senses reading, the wisdom extracting, the vagus nerve running between them as both physical pathway and energetic connection.

But the same principle of mind-partnership operates throughout the body's architecture. Consider Muladhara and Anahata — root and heart.

Muladhara is the foundation mind, governing survival, security, grounding, tribe. Its physical territory is the base of the body — the gut, the pelvic floor, the root structures that anchor everything above. Anahata is the bridge between soul and spirit, the heart mind where love transcends what soul-beings can know and compassion transcends what they naturally have. Its physical territory includes the heart organ itself and — significantly — the thymus gland, the organ that educates immune cells to distinguish self from non-self.

The thymus sits behind the sternum, at the heart centre. It is where T-cells — the immune system's most sophisticated defenders — learn to recognise which cells belong to the body and which are foreign. This is immunological identity. The organ that teaches the body to know itself sits at Anahata, the mind that teaches the soul to know its spiritual nature.

And the immune system's raw material — the lymphocytes, the gut-associated lymphoid tissue that contains roughly seventy percent of the body's immune cells — sits at Muladhara territory. The gut. The root. The foundation.

Root provides the raw material. Heart educates it. Muladhara grounds. Anahata elevates. The immune system reflects the energy teaching in physical architecture: rooted in the body's most fundamental tissue, educated in the organ that sits at the bridge between soul and spirit.

This is not analogy. This is anatomy mirroring energy structure — the same convergence this series has found everywhere it has looked: the vagus connecting throat to genitals, the sinuses manufacturing the same molecule as a billion-dollar pharmaceutical, the moaning body and the humming practitioner stimulating the same nerve. The physical body and the energy body are not separate systems. They are the same system, described in two languages.

## When the Light Goes Down

The evening requires one thing the modern world has made almost impossible: darkness.

Melatonin, the hormone that initiates sleep and serves as one of the body's most potent antioxidants, is suppressed by light. Specifically, by the blue-spectrum light that screens, LEDs, and overhead lighting produce in abundance. Bright light exposure between roughly ten at night and four in the morning suppresses dopamine, disrupts blood sugar regulation, and degrades sleep architecture. The body needs darkness the way it needs morning light — they are two halves of the same circadian requirement.

Dim the lights after sunset. Move the lamps low — below eye level, amber rather than blue. Let the body's melatonin begin its rise naturally, the way it has done every evening since the first vertebrate crawled onto land and discovered that the world went dark at night.

And before sleep — the OM hum again. The same sixty seconds. The sinuses flush. The vagus fires. The parasympathetic system engages. The cerebrospinal fluid oscillates. The body prepares for the night's maintenance work — the glymphatic waste clearance, the memory consolidation, the immune housekeeping, the restoration that sleep exists to provide.

The hum at dawn opens the day. The hum at dusk closes it. Bookends. The same practice, serving the same systems, creating the conditions under which the body — and everything that lives within it — can do the work it was designed to do.

## The Radical Simplicity

Here is the udbhu-kanti of it.

Every system this series has mapped — every mechanism, every pathway, every convergence — was already present. The sinuses were manufacturing nitric oxide before you knew what nitric oxide was. The vagus nerve was waiting for the vibration of a hum. The vocal cords were ready to enforce the breathing ratio. The olfactory system was reading immune compatibility before the first human understood what an immune system was. The skin was broadcasting your molecular identity to every living thing that came close enough to sense it.

All of it was there. All of it is there now.

What we have added, in the modern era, is interference. Products that strip the microbiome. Chemicals that disrupt the endocrine system. Fragrances that erase the chemical signature. Foods that poison the gut civilisation. Light at the wrong time. Darkness at the wrong time. Speed where slowness was needed. Silence where sound was trying to emerge. Shoes between the feet and the earth. Walls between the eyes and the sky.

The industries that sell these interferences are not conspiracies. They are commerce — the natural consequence of a system that profits from replacing what the body already provides with products that must be repurchased. Soap replaces sebum, so you need moisturiser. Antibacterial wash destroys the microbiome, so you need deodorant. Deodorant blocks the pores, so you need antiperspirant. Antiperspirant contains aluminium, so you need a "natural" alternative. Each product creates the market for the next one. The body, left alone, needed none of them.

Udbhu-kanti is not primitivism. It is not a demand to abandon modernity and live in a hut. It is the recognition that the simple, the real, and the sufficient contain more beauty, more function, and more intelligence than the complicated, the synthetic, and the excessive. The well-maintained wooden table. The bread made from flour and time. The skin that smells like itself. The morning that begins with light and sound and contact with the ground.

This is what Wayism has always taught. The body is not the obstacle. It is the temple — ten minds, trillions of residents, chemical communication systems millions of years old, pathways connecting throat to genitals and root to heart, sinuses manufacturing medicine, skin broadcasting identity, gut bacteria making happiness, a nervous system capable of ecstasy and maintenance through the same sixty-second hum.

You are not a synthetic sanitised simulation of a human. You are a human. The most sophisticated sensory instrument in the known universe, incarnated in one of the most sensuous organic bodies in the galaxy — and that is by divine design. Not an accident. Not a test of resistance to pleasure. Curriculum. Every sensation an opportunity. Every system already in place.

Want to live this human life thoroughly? Then live it as a human. Hum before dawn. Walk barefoot when you can. Let the morning light into your eyes. Wash with real soap and let your skin be what it is. Eat food your great-grandmother would recognise. Let your lover taste you, not your products. Make the sounds your body wants to make. Take your time. Dim the lights when the sun goes down. Hum again before sleep.

The body already contains everything it needs. It has been containing it since before you were born. The mechanisms were running before you learned their names.

All of it was there. Waiting for you to stop interfering.
