We do that, and it is not a balanced view. Here's why.

When you read *The Authentia* it is clear there are trillions upon trillions of soul beings in the universe. They come in many species, and all have different minds and faculties. The most advanced soul beings have communities with hierarchical power structures, governance structures, and religions. The smallest percentage of most advanced soul beings may be attracted to the Butterfly Path with its promise of developing the soul into an immortal spiritual being. Compared to the number of soul beings in the universe, there are few spiritual beings in the Spiritual Domain.

---

## Why would soul beings be interested in developing into spiritual beings?

**The first reason** is the matter of immortality. Soul beings are subject to the metaphysical Law of Samsara, which means they incarnate perpetually. They are forever incarnated into organic bodies in the Material Domain. They spend time in Soul Domain between incarnations, but at least half of their existence — which is not eternal — they will be incarnated in some or another natural body, living as that type of animal. According to *The Authentia*, many of those "animals" are advanced, even more so than humans, and have large kingdoms on planets scattered all over the galaxy. Advanced souls who become drawn to the idea of being a spiritual being — a deity — are mostly driven by the desire to be free of the Law of Samsara; to become immortal beings who do not necessarily incarnate into animal bodies.

**The second reason** is the matter of goodness. Unlike soul beings, spiritual beings are by nature "good" and exceptionally wise. Soul beings are by nature, and out of necessity, power-seeking beings who are well versed in competition, hoarding resources, competing for social standing and recognition, and they need the protection of their group and tribe to survive. Soul beings are naturally geared toward group-think, exploiting resources and those not of their tribe, and so on.

Spiritual beings, on the other hand, have a different function in the galaxy and are ideally equipped for that function. They are managers of energy structures and maintainers of the energy flows in the galaxy. The spiritual beings of our species are helpers and healers. Our species of spiritual being live in the sphere of existence called Sukhavati (Sanskrit: Land of Pure Bliss, or Land of Pure Light). We are perhaps the most powerful spiritual beings of all species because of our exceptional wisdom and powers. Because of the work we do in Soul Domain and Material Domain — helping other beings when necessary to intervene on the metaphysical level — we need exceptional wisdom and energy manipulation abilities. And we are developing those in the Butterfly Path school.

We are the only spiritual species who have lived as soul beings, and build on that experience the wisdom and skills necessary to become immortal deities.

The structures that keep the galaxy need maintenance because natural things and soul beings often disrupt balances, which must be repaired. Our species plays a role in maintaining the galaxy, with particular emphasis on interpersonal assistance to animals and souls.

---

## So why do humans seem to denigrate soul nature?

It is not so much "humans" who are guilty of that — senior students on the Butterfly Path are the more guilty. Why is this?

It is most probably because we have struggled to overcome lower-self minds for many human lifetimes. Let's put a hypothetical number on this just to explain. Let's assume that the ancient are right in saying that each chakra has 7 x 7 main functions — that is 7 mains with 7 sub-functions under each main function, giving each chakra 49 main functions.

Each of the 49 main chakra mind functions interact with any of the 49 functions of its neighbouring chakras. And each time a new mental scenario is created, another ability of the soul emerges — along with another potential problem the soul may encounter. The non-human soul, or the advanced soul not on Butterfly Path, has 49 to the power of 3 such potential scenarios that the soul minds deal with. 49 to the power of 3 equals 117,649 mental issues that soul can encounter.

When that soul incarnates as a human it is because it was enrolled on the Butterfly Path. It receives one more chakra mind at once — the Anahata Chakra. This mind introduces a host of diverse types of love that the soul has never had to deal with before. That is the start of its development toward becoming a spiritual being. Later, when Anahata is fully engaged, the soul will activate its spiritual chakras, and three more higher-self chakras are added.

The introduction of Anahata to the junior student's soul adds more complexity. Now we are looking at 49 to the power of 4 mental scenarios and abilities. 49 to the power of 4 equals 5,764,801 mental pathways and skills that the soul can manage.

---

Back to our question — why do senior students on the Path speak down about soul minds?

It is really only because we struggled so hard, for so many lifetimes, to free ourselves from the power those minds held over us. Like an addiction, we had to struggle to rid ourselves of their influence on our spiritual development. We had to sanctify each one of them, and had to — there's no exact equivalent in aethereal energy but you understand what is meant — reprogram each of those mental faculties to think like a spirit, no longer thinking like a soul.

It was a considerable struggle. Now, like a recovering addict, we have profound respect for the power of those energies. We see the power of it all around us because junior students who are battling those drives are very vocal and visible in society. They create the soul-type religions, criminal organisations, usurp nations, and build tyrannical empires — embracing every new vogue idea that is fed to them and prepared to dismantle the lives of all who do not agree.

But soul minds, for souls that are not on the Butterfly Path, are not evil or bad. They are simply perfect. To exist as a soul being in soul environments, incarnating into new worlds and new bodies all the time, you need exactly the skills of soul minds.

Therefore, if our literature sometimes seems disparaging about soul minds, it is because we have great respect for the destructive power of those drives in the human context.

---

There is one more dimension worth naming. As we mature on the Butterfly Path, we become increasingly "good" by nature — we seek more peace and wholesomeness, we become more helpful, kind, simple, and humble. Junior students operating from soul-mind nature tend to read those qualities as weakness, as opportunity. They are accustomed to competing for resources and sustenance, and a senior student who radiates openness and generosity can become a target.

This same dynamic plays out at the level of human societies. Idealistic systems built on the assumption that most participants will contribute rather than exploit tend to run into predictable difficulty — not because care for others or equity is wrong, but because systems built around assumptions of spiritual maturity will always be tested by the soul-mind drives of the majority. We see this pattern repeat across history, regardless of the ideology involved. The fault lies not in the ideal but in the mismatch between the ideal and the actual stage of development of those participating in it.

This is why it is a fine line we must walk as we learn how to be helpers and healers of beings who have soul nature — not naive, not dismissive, but clear-eyed about what we are working with and patient enough to work with it wisely.
