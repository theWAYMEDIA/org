## Wayist Insight

This is the comprehensive teaching dharani of Wayism — its entire cosmology sung in a single piece. The Sanskrit carries the full doctrine; the English verse walks through it as a teaching, line by line, making the meaning explicit.

The dharani moves through four teachings:

**The Energy Teaching** — *Om tattva-śakti vyāpako'khilam / Sarveṣu caitanyaṃ pratiṣṭhitam.* The energies of THAT (tattva-śakti) pervade everything (vyāpako'khilam). In all beings, consciousness (caitanya) is established. This is the foundation: the universe is not dead matter animated by spirit — it is energy all the way through, and consciousness is woven into its fabric.

**The Neighbour Teaching** — *Caitanyaṃ tattvajaṃ sarvatra / Jīva-saṃghāḥ samantataḥ.* Consciousness born from THAT abounds everywhere. Living communities (jīva-saṃghāḥ) surround us on all sides. Everything is alive, everything is our neighbour — not as a moral commandment but as a statement of reality. We live among communities of conscious beings.

**The Mahavakya Dialog** — *Tat tvam asi, So'ham.* Two great sayings in conversation. Tat tvam asi (That Thou Art) is Source speaking to the individual: "You are of My nature." So'ham (I Am That) is the individual's response: "I recognise my Source-nature." In Wayist understanding, this is participatory relationship, not identity-collapse. Source says "You are of Me." The soul responds "I am of You." The closing — Śakti-rūpāḥ sarve'ham Om (We are all forms of energy, all are OM) — lands in the plural. Not "I alone am That" but "we are all forms of that energy."

**The Divine Family** — *Amitābha pitā divyaloke / Pāṇḍarajanani mātā divyaloke.* Now the teaching names names. Amitābha Father in the divine realm. Pāṇḍarajanani Mother in the divine realm. These are not metaphors for Source — they are specific spiritual beings who govern Sukhāvatī, the heaven where human souls on the Butterfly Path graduate. The closing — Ātma-jyotiḥ prāṇa-śaktiḥ (Spirit-light and soul-energy) — names what we are: spirit-light in heaven's keeping, soul-energy beings walking the earth.

The mahavakya repeats after the divine family naming, now carrying fuller meaning: when we chant "That Thou Art, I Am That" after naming our Father and Mother God, the dialog becomes familial. It is not cosmic abstraction. It is a child recognising its parents' nature within itself.
