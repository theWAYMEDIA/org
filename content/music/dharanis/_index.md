## What Is a Dharani?

A dharani is an extended sacred formula — longer than a mantra, sometimes running to many verses. The word is Sanskrit: *dhāraṇī*, from *dhṛ*, to hold or sustain. A dharani holds and sustains: it holds the mind in sustained attentiveness, and it sustains a devotional connection across the duration of its recitation.

Where a mantra condenses a teaching into a seed-phrase, a dharani unfolds a teaching across many lines. Both are devotional tools; they work differently. A mantra is suited to continuous background repetition. A dharani asks for a dedicated period of sitting, standing, or walking recitation — full engagement for its full duration.

Dharanis in the Wayist heritage are drawn primarily from the ancient Mahamarga and DaoFa streams. Many address Divine Tara figures directly. Some encode cosmological teachings about the three domains in the form of protective recitation. All are grounded in the understanding that sound, when given with intention and understanding, is an act of alignment with theWAY.

---

## How to Use Dharanis

**Daily recitation:** Choose one dharani as a standing practice. Learn it well enough that recitation becomes fluent — attention then shifts from decoding to attending to meaning and sound simultaneously.

**Devotional offering:** Dharanis can be recited as an offering to one's Divine Tara, to beings in difficulty, or as part of a community practice. The intention shapes the use.

**Study and contemplation:** The texts of longer dharanis reward careful reading separate from recitation. Many contain compressed philosophical teaching that opens slowly with repeated encounter.

It is not necessary to understand every word for recitation to have value. Understanding deepens practice; practice also deepens understanding. Both directions are valid.

## Dharanis in This Collection

### [Namo Amitābha Dharani](/music/dharanis/amitabha-dharani/)
The great Wayist invocation of both divine parents — Father God (Infinite Light) and Mother God (She Who Births in White) — moving through the nectar teaching to the climax of immortal sound.
*Sanskrit / English — Wayist Traditional*

### [Om Bhūr Bhuvaḥ Svaḥa](/music/dharanis/wayist-gayatri-dharani/)
The Wayist Gayatri dharani — the ancient Vedic light-invocation reframed within Wayist cosmology, addressing the three domains (earth, life, heaven) and the divine light that illumines consciousness. Recorded by Collectivo Mariposa.
*Sanskrit / English — Wayist Traditional*

### [Om Mārge Paragate](/music/dharanis/paragate-dharani/)
The Wayist Paragate dharani — "gone beyond, gone completely beyond" — the great crossing teaching of the Butterfly Path expressed as extended recitation. Recorded by Collectivo Mariposa.
*Sanskrit / English — Wayist Traditional*

### [Om Mārge Paragate — Spanish](/music/dharanis/paragate-dharani-spanish/)
The Paragate dharani in Spanish — for Caminista practitioners and Spanish-speaking Wayist communities.
*Sanskrit / Spanish — Wayist Traditional*

### [Om Yesu Dharani](/music/dharanis/om-yesu-dharani/)
The Energy Abounds teaching dharani — honouring Lord Yesu's teaching that divine energy permeates all things, all beings are conscious neighbours, and "So Ham" (I Am That) is the recognition at the heart of Wayism. Performed by Colectivo Mariposa.
*Sanskrit / English — Wayist Traditional*

### [Om Mārge Sarva Om — English](/music/dharanis/om-marge-sarva-om-english/)
The full teaching dharani: "In THE WAY, All Are OM" — a comprehensive theological statement of Wayist understanding of energy, consciousness, divine family, and universal kinship. Line by line instruction.
*Sanskrit / English — © elCamino de Caminismo A.C.*

### [Om Mārge Sarva Om — German](/music/dharanis/om-marge-sarva-om-german/)

*Sanskrit / German — © elCamino de Caminismo A.C.*

### [Om Mārge Sarva Om — Spanish](/music/dharanis/om-marge-sarva-om-spanish/)
"En EL CAMINO, Todos Somos OM" — the full teaching dharani in Spanish. For Caminista and Latin American Wayist communities.
*Sanskrit / Spanish — © elCamino de Caminismo A.C.*

### [Ven a la Madre](/music/dharanis/ven-a-la-madre/)
"Come to the Mother" — a Caminista dharani honouring Yesu and Mari, the sacred sound calling to Mother God. Performed by Collectivo Mariposa. Lyrics by Claudette Sky.
*Sanskrit / Spanish — Wayist Traditional*

### [Ehi Elaya Ramaya](/music/dharanis/poem-from-the-cross/)
"Poem from the Cross" — Yesu's last sacred utterances as dharani. The Aramaic cry *Ehi Elaya* ("my God, my God") understood within Wayist teaching as a call to Mother God. Performed by Collectivo Mariposa. Lyrics by Claudette Sky.
*Sanskrit / English — Wayist Traditional*
