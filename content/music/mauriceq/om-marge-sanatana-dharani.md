## Wayist Insight

This is one of the oldest dharanis preserved in the Wayist lineage — three short lines in Sanskrit that have been chanted by people of the Way across continents and centuries. It is short enough that a child can learn it and old enough that whole worldviews lean on it. Maya Sabda and MauriceQ each carry it in their own register; the dharani itself does the teaching, and the song works in either voice.

### The Three Lines

**Om purāṇe mārge sthitāḥ** — *Om, established in the ancient Path.* The verb to notice is **sthitāḥ** — *established, set, already standing.* It is past participle, completed action. We are not trying to find the Path. We are not auditioning to be allowed onto it. We are *already standing* on it, and have been since before this body. **Purāṇa** means *ancient* in the deepest sense — primordial, predating all named religions. The Path is older than every tradition that has ever pointed at it.

**Mahāmārgo gatir divyā** — *The Great Way is the divine course.* **Mahāmārga** is the Great Path — the through-line that runs underneath all the local trails human cultures have marked. **Gati** is *course, motion, the going itself.* The Path is not a place you arrive at; it is the going of every soul through embodied learning. **Divyā** — *divine* — places this going within the spiritual order, not outside it. Soul-school is not separate from the divine plan; it *is* the divine plan in motion.

**Sanātana paramparā svaḥa** — *Eternal transmission unto heaven.* **Sanātana** means *eternal, perpetual, without origin in time.* **Paramparā** is the unbroken lineage — literally *one after another* — the chain of teachers and learners that carries truth across generations. The teaching that has always been is handed forward, person to person, until each soul reaches the realm of light. Not bestowed by an institution. Not gatekept by a priesthood. Passed.

### What These Three Lines Imply

There is a quiet argument living inside this dharani, and it is one of Wayism's most consequential teachings. *If* a Father-Mother God genuinely wants a spiritual path for humans, *then* that Path must be available to every human who has ever lived or ever will. Not only the literate. Not only the well-fed. Not only those born into the right tradition. *Every* human — including the infant who lived three days, the cognitively impaired adult who never grasped a doctrine, the slave who never had a free hour to contemplate, the lifelong member of a religion that never named the Wayist God by the right syllables, the dying who could not speak. If the Path is divine and the divine is inclusive, the Path cannot be contingent on resources humans variably possess. The Sanskrit line **purāṇe mārge sthitāḥ** carries this implication in its grammar: you are *already established*. You did not enrol. You arrived enrolled.

This is why Wayism does not seek converts. Many traditions assume the Path is *theirs* and that humans must come to it through their gate. Wayism reads the geography differently: the **Mahāmārga** runs under every tradition, surfacing here clearly, there in fragments, elsewhere obscured. Religions are local maps of varying accuracy. The Path is the territory. People you love who walk other traditions are walking the same Mahāmārga, possibly with worse maps, but on the same ground.

The closing of the song states the teaching plainly: *All humans are on the one Great Path. The Way.* That is the dharani's whole heart, sung in English so a child can carry it. **Sanātana paramparā** — the eternal transmission — passes from Maya's voice to MauriceQ's to yours to whoever sings it next. One after another. That is how the Path has always moved, and how it always will.
