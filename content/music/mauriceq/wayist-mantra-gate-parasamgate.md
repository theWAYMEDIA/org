## Wayist Insight

Anyone who has spent time near Buddhist meditation halls will recognise the syllables at the heart of this dharani. *Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā* — the famous closing mantra of the Heart Sutra, chanted in temples from Tibet to Japan for more than a thousand years. The Wayist version keeps the spine of those syllables and bends them in ways that some Sanskrit scholars find irritating. We honour their concern and chant on. This is received material in the Wayist lineage; the bearded scholar may chant his version while we sing ours, and the Path will recognise both walkers.

### Where Wayism Bends the Inherited Form

**oṃ te yathāgate** — *Om, to You who has come thus.* The standard mantra describes a journey; the Wayist mantra addresses someone. **Te** — *to you* — turns the chant from a meditation on the path into a song offered to a person at the path's end. From the first syllable, the Wayist version refuses solitary contemplation. The journey is relational.

**gāte parāgate parasaṅgate** — *gone, gone beyond, gone utterly beyond.* This is the original mantra's spine, kept intact. The progression matters: *gate* (gone), *parāgate* (gone beyond), *parasaṅgate* (gone completely beyond — beyond even the beyond). Three crossings, each more total than the last.

**bodhini svāhā** — *to Her, the Awakener — it is so.* The standard reading is **bodhi** (awakening, neuter) — the destination as a state. The Wayist reading is **bodhini** (feminine) — the Awakener as a Person. The journey ends not in a state but in a meeting. She is the divine feminine wisdom — the Princess Prajñāpāramitā in the Wayist divine family — and the dharani arrives at her feet.

### The Middle Term

The closing line of the song states the whole teaching plainly: *From ignorance through love to the furthest shore.* Notice the middle word. Buddhist commentators usually have the journey turn on **prajñā** — wisdom, insight, seeing-through. Wayism inserts **love** in that pivot position. The English verse spells it out: *Once ignorant, my soul found love / Awareness came like light above.* Love comes first; awareness follows. The heart opens, and only then can the mind expand.

This carries an entire Wayist anthropology. The soul journeying is not a disembodied awareness solving the puzzle of existence; it is a being learning to love. The heart chakra — **Anāhata**, *the unstruck* — is the portal between the lower self and the higher self. Without love the bridge between soul and spirit is not crossed. Paul knew this when he wrote that without love we become as sounding brass. The mantra puts love in the middle of the journey because love is anatomically the middle of the journey.

### The Feminine Awakener

The shift from **bodhi** to **bodhini** makes her explicit: *bodhini svāhā* — *to Her, the Awakener, hail.* The dharani becomes a love-song offered to the divine feminine. The Princess is not a symbol; she is She, and one bows to her by name. Heaven, like all of life, has a Father and a Mother. Wisdom — Sophia, Prajñā — does not arrive in the lecture; she arrives later, layer by silent layer, until one ordinary day you simply know.

MauriceQ and Collectivo Mariposa carry it in different registers — the intimate lounge voice and the ceremonial ensemble — and both work, because the dharani itself is doing the teaching. *From ignorance through love to the furthest shore.* That single line is the whole journey.
