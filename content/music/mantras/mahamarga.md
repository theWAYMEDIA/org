# Om Mārge Mahāmārga
**The Great Path Mantra**

## Lyrics

Om purāṇe mārge sthitāḥ
Mahāmārgo gatir divyā
Sanātana paramparā svaḥa

OM...
All humans are on the one
Great Path.
The Way.

---

## Wayist Insight

This is the identity mantra — the chant that declares who Wayists understand themselves to be and where they stand. Used by millions around the world, it helps practitioners meditate on the many lives they have walked the Great Path, and on the teachers and divine guides who have accompanied them along the Way.

Each line deepens the claim:

**Om purāṇe mārge sthitāḥ** — *Established in the ancient path.* The word purāṇe (ancient) matters. Wayism does not understand itself as a new religion or a modern innovation. The Way is the oldest path — the one that all authentic spiritual traditions draw from. And sthitāḥ (established, standing firm) says we are not searching for the path. We are already on it. We have been on it across lifetimes.

**Mahāmārgo gatir divyā** — *The Great Way is the divine course.* Mahāmārga — the Great Path — is another name for THE WAY itself, the organised system of metaphysical laws through which all souls develop. Gatir (course, movement, destination) tells us this path goes somewhere. It is not circular. Divyā (divine) reminds us its origin and destination are heavenly.

**Sanātana paramparā svaḥa** — *Eternal transmission, unto heaven.* Sanātana means eternal, timeless — this teaching has no beginning and no expiry. Paramparā means transmission, the unbroken chain of teacher to student across ages. The mantra honours every teacher who has ever carried THE WAY forward, from the ancient rishis through Lord Jesus to the guides who serve today.

The closing English lines carry a quietly radical claim: *all* humans are on this one Great Path. Not only Wayists, not only the spiritually inclined — everyone. The Way is not something you join. It is something you are already walking.
