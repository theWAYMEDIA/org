## Wayist Insight

This mantra places the practitioner exactly where every human stands — between past and future, neither origin nor endpoint, a living link in the chain of transmission.

**Om pitrṇām jñānam āyāti** — *The ancestors' wisdom comes.* The wisdom of those who walked before us (pitrṇām jñānam) arrives — not through supernatural means but through texts, practices, and realised beings who preserved it. The earthy, humming sound of pitrṇām (of the ancestors/fathers) is ancient and grounding. This wisdom is not invented. It comes to us.

**Dhāraye samarpaye ca** — *I hold it and I offer it forward.* Two duties in parallel: dhāraye (to hold firmly, to understand deeply) and samarpaye (to offer forward, to pass on). We are not the owners of this teaching but its temporary custodians. What we receive, we must both comprehend and transmit.

**Paramparā ciraṃ jīvatu svaḥa** — *May the transmission live long, unto heaven.* The mantra closes as a blessing — ciraṃ jīvatu (may it live long) — directed at the paramparā itself, the unbroken stream of transmission. It is the lineage we are praying for, not ourselves. Svaḥa seals it as an offering upward.

This is not ancestor worship. Wayism honours the chain of transmission without deifying the transmitters. We stand between those who carried the teaching before us and those who will carry it after, serving something larger than any individual life.
