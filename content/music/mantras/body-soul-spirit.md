## Lyrics

## Wayist Insight

This mantra teaches one of the most fundamental distinctions in Wayist understanding: the human being is composed of three domains — body, soul, and spirit — and these three remain distinct. They cooperate, they develop together, but they do not collapse into one.

The Sanskrit builds this lesson in two beautiful parallel lines before drawing the conclusion in the third:

**Om bhūmi prāṇa jyotiḥ ca** — *Earth, breath, and light.* These are the three energies: bhūmi (earth/matter — the physical domain), prāṇa (breath/life-force — the psychic domain), and jyotiḥ (light — the spiritual domain). The mantra begins not with the person but with the energy fields we inhabit.

**Kāya jīva ātman ca** — *Body, soul, and spirit.* Now the three aspects of the being itself: kāya (the physical body), jīva (the soul — the living, learning self), and ātman (spirit — our eternal nature). Each dwells in its corresponding energy domain.

**Tribhiḥ saha jīvāmi svaḥa** — *With all three I live, unto heaven.* Here is the Wayist commitment: I live and strive *with* all three, not seeking to transcend the body or dissolve the soul into spirit. Growth happens through the integration of all three working together on the Butterfly Path.

This matters because many spiritual traditions teach that the goal is to shed the lower domains — to escape the body, transcend the mind, merge into pure spirit. Wayism teaches the opposite: all three are needed, all three are honoured, and transformation happens through their cooperation, not their elimination.
