## Wayist Insight

This mantra carries the central metaphor of Wayist spirituality: the Butterfly Path. In three Sanskrit lines it maps the entire journey of the soul — from earth through transformation to heaven — using the caterpillar, the chrysalis, and the butterfly.

**Om bhūmau jīvana ātmani** — *In earth, in life, in spirit.* The three domains named in sequence: bhūmau (on earth — the physical), jīvana (in life — the soul, the living self), ātmani (in spirit — the eternal nature). This is where the journey takes place, across all three at once.

**Kīṭa kośa titibhaḥ ca** — *Caterpillar, chrysalis, and butterfly.* The three stages of transformation: kīṭa (the caterpillar — the earthbound, learning creature), kośa (the chrysalis — the sheath of radical change), and titibhaḥ (the butterfly — the being that emerges with wings). Each maps to a stage of soul development.

**Mahāmārge pariṇāmaḥ svaḥa** — *On the Great Path, transformation, unto heaven.* The conclusion draws it all together. Pariṇāmaḥ (transformation) is not a metaphor here — it is the actual process. On the Mahāmārga, the Great Path, souls genuinely transform from one kind of being into another, and svaḥa sends this truth upward as an offering.

What makes this teaching distinctly Wayist is what it does *not* say. It does not say we are already butterflies who have forgotten our wings. It does not say the caterpillar is an illusion. The caterpillar is real, the chrysalis is real, and the messy, difficult, beautiful process of transformation is the whole point of being here. Souls are in school. The butterfly is what we are becoming — not what we secretly already are.
